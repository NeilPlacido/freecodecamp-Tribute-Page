Chadwick Boseman

The King of Wakanda aka King T'Challa aka Black Panther

The Legacy Time Line

  • 1995 - He graduated from T. L. Hanna High School.
  • 1998 - He attended the Oxford Summer Program of the British American Drama Academy at Balliol College, Oxford, in England.
  • 2000 - He was recruited to play basketball at college but chose the arts instead, attending college at Howard University in Washington, D.C. and graduating.
  • 2008 - Moved to Los Angeles to pursue his film and acting career.
  • 2010 - Landed his first regular role in the television series Persons Unknown as the Marine Graham McNair.
  • 2013 - Had a breakthrough role with the film 42, in which he portrayed the lead role of baseball legend Jackie Robinson.
  • 2014 - Boseman starred in another sporting film, Draft Day, as fictional football player Vontae Mack.
  • 2016 - He starred as Thoth, a deity from Egyptian mythology, in Gods of Egypt.
  • 2016 - Also began portraying the Marvel Comics character T'Challa / Black Panther in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Captain America: Civil War was his first film in a five-picture deal with Marvel Entertainment.
  • 2018 - Returned as the Black Panther in his original movie Black Panther.
  • 2018 - Appeared again in the marvels Avenger: Infinity War.
  • 2019 - Last appearance in the Avengers: Endgame the highest-most grossing movie of all time.
  • 2020 - Boseman on their list of the 10 Best Movie Performances for both Da 5 Bloods and Ma Rainey.
  • 2021 - Voiced the alternate versions of T'Challa in the Disney+ animated series.
